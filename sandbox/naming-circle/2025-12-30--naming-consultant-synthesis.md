## Cross-Term Patterns Discovered

### Pattern A: The Measurement/Truth Gap (STRONGEST)
Three domains independently articulated that **quantitative measures fail to capture qualitative reality**:
- Coverage Vertigo - high metrics masking uncertainty
- Metric Mirage - green dashboards hiding pathology
- Assertion Saturation - more checks not always better

**This suggests collective epistemic maturity about the limits of measurement.**

### Pattern B: The Grief Family
Both Optimization Grief and Contract-Grief acknowledge technical work carries emotional weight:
- Optimization Grief: mourning beautiful solutions that must die
- Contract-Grief: mourning promises that must be broken

**Validates the experiential turn in vocabulary.**

### Pattern C: The Vertigo Family
"Vertigo" emerging as word for epistemic disorientation:
- Coverage Vertigo (metric/intuition gap)
- Interface Vertigo (multiple valid perspectives)
- Threshold Shimmer (boundary oscillation)
