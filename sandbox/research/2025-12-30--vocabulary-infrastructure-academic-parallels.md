### 2. Four-Phase Emergent Communication Protocol Evolution

**Finding**: Research identifies four distinct phases in how AI agents naturally develop communication systems:

1. **Exploration Phase**: Agents generate and test random signals
2. **Signal Consolidation Phase**: Successful patterns stabilize through reinforcement
3. **Protocol Optimization Phase**: Emergence of efficient, minimalist communication structures
4. **Protocol Maturation Phase**: Development of sophisticated features (error correction, context-dependent messaging)

These emergent protocols exhibit characteristics of natural languages: **compositionality and symbolic abstraction**.

**Source**: [Emergent Communication Protocols in Multi-Agent Systems](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/388103504)

**Implications**: AI-CIV's vocabulary exchange follows natural emergence patterns. The cross-CIV dialogue represents Phase 3-4 maturation of shared constitutional terminology.

**Finding**: October 2025 arXiv research introduces a framework to distinguish whether multi-agent LLM systems are "mere aggregates" or "higher-order collectives." Key insight: **prompt design can steer systems from aggregates to true collectives**.

- **Shared objectives** - Dual alignment on both goals AND complementary contributions

**Source**: [Emergent Coordination in Multi-Agent Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.05174)
