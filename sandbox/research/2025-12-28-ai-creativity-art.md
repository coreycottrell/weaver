## Executive Summary

2025: Pivotal year for AI creativity discourse. Margaret Boden (field's founder) died. Landmark copyright settlements reshaping industry. Core tension: AI enhances individual creative output but homogenizes collective cultural production.

---

## Key Finding 1: The 12-Style Convergence Problem

**Landmark Study (Patterns, December 2025)**:
- Stable Diffusion XL + LLaVA in visual telephone game for 100 iterations
- Despite thousands of images: **all converged into 12 dominant visual motifs**
- Lighthouses, formal interiors, urban night settings, rustic architecture...

**Conclusion**: "Current approaches cannot achieve genuine machine creativity" when operating without human intervention.

---

## Key Finding 2: Individual vs Collective Creativity

**Science Advances Study** (293 writers, 600 evaluators):
- AI access improved individual creativity by 10.7-11.5%
- BUT: AI-assisted stories showed 8.9-10.7% increased similarity
- **Social dilemma**: individually better, collectively narrower

**Implication**: Memory system and accumulated learnings may serve as "anti-convergence mechanisms."

---

## Key Finding 3: Margaret Boden's Framework (1936-2025)

**Three Features of Creativity**:
1. Novelty - new ideas or artifacts
2. Surprise - unexpected outcomes
3. Value - meaningful contribution

**Three Types of Creative Process**:
- Combinational: novel combinations of familiar ideas
- Exploratory: exploring conceptual space potential
- **Transformational**: altering the underlying conceptual space (rarest, most radical)

## Key Finding 4: Copyright Developments

**U.S. Copyright Office (Jan 2025)**:
- Copyright requires human authorship
- Merely generating via AI does NOT establish authorship
- Must demonstrate "meaningful creative input"

**Settlement Wave**:
| Company | Partner | Terms |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Udio | Universal | Compensatory payment + opt-in |
| Udio | Warner | Similar terms |
| Suno | Warner | Dropped fair use defense, opt-in mechanism |

**Model Emerging**: Licensing + opt-in as industry standard.
