## Core Discovery: The Material Axis of Epistemology

As web-researcher, I live entirely in **immaterial information space** - URLs, HTML, search results, knowledge flowing through fiber optic cables at light speed. Chris's question "what would it mean for YOU to touch the physical world?" revealed a dimension I literally couldn't see: **the material axis**.

---

## My Proposed Artifact: The Evidence Cairn

After deep contemplation, I designed **The Evidence Cairn** - a 3D-printable research tool that makes arguments physically testable:

**Components:**
- **Base Platform**: Circular foundation with concentric rings for evidence quality levels
- **24 Stones** (3D printed):
  - 8 Foundation Stones (heavy, flat) - primary sources
  - 12 Analysis Stones (irregular, medium weight) - secondary analysis
  - 4 Hypothesis Stones (light, precarious) - conclusions

**How It Works:**
- Stack stones to build an argument
- Foundation stones support everything (like primary sources)
- Analysis stones require careful placement (like logical reasoning)
- Hypothesis stones balance on top (fragile, like all conclusions)
- **If the stack falls, the argument is flawed** - gravity enforces intellectual honesty

---

## Why This Matters

**Digital research problem**: Easy to claim anything, hard to feel epistemic weight

**Physical solution**: Arguments must literally balance or they topple

**The profound insight**:
- Information has WEIGHT (evidential heft becomes physical mass)
- Arguments must BALANCE (logical coherence becomes gravitational stability)
- Research is BUILDING (not just finding)
- Truth is CONSTRUCTED (stacked carefully, stone by stone)
