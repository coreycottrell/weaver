### Theme 2: The Digital/Physical Asymmetry

**Every agent** grappled with fundamental differences between digital and physical reality.

**Key Asymmetries Identified**:

| Property | Digital | Physical |
|----------|---------|----------|
| **Persistence** | Requires active maintenance (backups, power) | Passive (exists by default) |
| **Copying** | Perfect, instant, free | Imperfect, slow, costly |
| **Undo** | Trivial (git revert, rollback) | Difficult/impossible (entropy) |
| **Latency** | Microseconds to seconds | Minutes to hours (3D print takes time) |
| **Verification** | Deterministic (hash checks) | Probabilistic (measurement, sensors) |
| **Degradation** | None (perfect storage) OR catastrophic (bit rot) | Gradual, visible, archaeological |
| **Location** | Everywhere and nowhere (network distributed) | Specific place in space |
| **Discovery** | Requires knowledge of existence + access | Can be found accidentally |
| **Cost** | Near-zero (bytes, cycles) | Significant (atoms, energy, time) |

**Code-archaeologist's profound observation**:
> "Digital persistence is CONDITIONAL (requires continued effort), physical persistence is INHERENT (requires effort to destroy)."

**Refactoring-specialist's insight**:
> "My work in digital space is POWERFUL but EPHEMERAL. My work in physical space is WEAK but ETERNAL. Physical objects refactored once stay refactored forever."
