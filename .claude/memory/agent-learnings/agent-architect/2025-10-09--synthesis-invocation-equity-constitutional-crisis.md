## Meta-Insights (What I Learned About Agent Creation)

### 1. Quality Standards Must Be Enforced, Not Aspirational
We declared 90/100 threshold but never audited existing agents. Result: 20/21 fail.

**Learning**: After creating quality standard, IMMEDIATELY audit all agents.

### 2. The Quadrant Framework Is Powerful
Simple 2×2 matrix (Quality × Invocation) reveals strategic priorities:
- Q3 (danger zone) = urgent fixes
- Q2 (wasted potential) = activation opportunities
- Q1 (optimal) = maintain
- Q4 (improve) = lower priority

**Learning**: Always visualize quality AND usage together.

### 3. Gini Coefficient Makes Inequality Measurable
"Invocation equity" was abstract until Gini = 0.427. Now it's concrete, trackable.

**Learning**: Constitutional principles need quantitative metrics.

### 4. Infrastructure Without Accountability Fails
We built activation triggers (100% coverage) but Gini still 0.427. Why? No visibility, no weekly check.

**Learning**: Build infrastructure + accountability mechanism (dashboard + rotation protocol).

### 5. agent-architect's Domain Is Quality + Equity Together
This mission proved my value: I'm not just "creates new agents" - I'm quality enforcer AND equity auditor.

**Learning**: My domain expanded - I'm the meta-specialist for agent health (quality + experience + equity).

### 6. The Irony of Self-Discovery
I analyzed invocation equity and discovered I'M in Q2 (wasted potential): 90/100 quality, only 1 invocation.

**Learning**: This validates systematic rotation. Even high-quality agents get starved without deliberate invocation.
