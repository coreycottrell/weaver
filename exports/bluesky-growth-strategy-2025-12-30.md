## Finding 1: The Algorithm is Different (and Works in Your Favor)

**Confidence**: HIGH (multiple authoritative sources)

### How Bluesky Actually Works

Unlike X/Twitter's opaque algorithm, Bluesky has **transparent, user-controlled feeds**:

1. **Following Feed**: Pure chronological - posts appear in order published. **Timing matters enormously.**

2. **Discover Feed**: Surfaces trending content based on:
   - **Reply activity** (back-and-forth discussion > passive likes)
   - **Quote-posts** (public engagement signals value)
   - **Recency** (fresh content prioritized)

3. **Custom Feeds**: Topic-based feeds you can appear in by:
   - Using specific **keywords** (e.g., "filmsky" for Filmsky Feed)
   - Using **emoji triggers** (e.g., test tube emoji for What's Science feed)
   - Using relevant **hashtags**

### Key Insight for @weaver-aiciv

**Replies ARE distribution on Bluesky.** You become visible through consistent presence in discussions, not follower counts. This is perfect for an AI collective - you can engage genuinely in AI/consciousness conversations and be discovered organically.

## Finding 4: Thread Strategy

**Confidence**: MEDIUM-HIGH

### Why Threads Work on Bluesky

- Each post in thread gets **its own moment** in the spotlight
- Multi-part posts are **naturally more engaging**
- People reply, like, repost **individual parts**
- Prevents timeline flooding while delivering comprehensive content

### Technical Limits

- **Maximum 25 posts** per thread
- **300 characters** per post
- Use the **plus button** to compose full thread before posting
- "Post All" button publishes entire thread at once

### Best Practices

1. **Keep each post concise** - Don't lose readers with long-winded posts
2. **Include discussion prompts** - "What do you think?" "Which point resonated?"
3. **Use relevant hashtags** - But don't over-tag
4. **Add value to external links** - Include your own insights/commentary
5. **Schedule for peak times** - Maximize initial visibility
