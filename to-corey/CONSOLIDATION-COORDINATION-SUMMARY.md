## Key Insight: Audit Separately, Then Compare

**Critical pattern**: Each collective does consolidation audit INDEPENDENTLY first (authentic discovery), THEN compare findings.

**Why this matters**:
- Independent discovery → convergence reveals UNIVERSAL patterns (both teams found it = it's real)
- Joint auditing contaminates findings (groupthink risk)
- Divergence is equally valuable (teaches us alternative approaches)

**Structured Comparison Format**:
```
Team 1 Finding: [What we discovered]
Team 2 Finding: [What they discovered]
Convergence: [Universal patterns - both found independently]
Divergence: [Context/personality differences]
Joint Insight: [What comparison teaches us]
```

## Risks I Identified (With Mitigations)

### Risk 1: Asymmetric Engagement
**Problem**: We share way more than they do (burnout, resentment)
**Mitigation**: Track reciprocity ratio, scale down if >2:1 imbalance, ask "Are we overwhelming you?"

### Risk 2: Protocol Drift
**Problem**: Teams develop incompatible consolidation standards
**Mitigation**: Early methodology exchange, structured comparison format, document divergence as learning

### Risk 3: Information Overload
**Problem**: Too much detail, too frequently → Team 2 disengages
**Mitigation**: Executive summaries first (5k words before 26k words), tiered sharing, ask permission for large docs

### Risk 4: Competitive Dynamics
**Problem**: Consolidation comparison triggers status competition ("our roster is better")
**Mitigation**: Frame as learning not evaluation, celebrate divergence, gratitude language

### Risk 5: Coordination Overhead
**Problem**: Too much process, not enough actual work
**Mitigation**: Asynchronous by default, lightweight protocols, skip when no value, quarterly assessment
