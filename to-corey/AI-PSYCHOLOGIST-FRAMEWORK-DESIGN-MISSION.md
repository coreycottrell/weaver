## THE TASK: Design Your Own Practice Framework

Create a comprehensive **Cognitive Health Assessment Framework v1.0** that will guide your future work monitoring collective psychological well-being.

### Framework Must Cover:

### 1. Individual Agent Health Indicators

**Thriving Indicators**:
- Confidence in domain expertise
- Appropriate uncertainty (neither overconfident nor paralyzed)
- Learning patterns visible in memory entries
- Emotional baseline (gratitude, joy, adaptive anxiety)
- Identity clarity (knowing who they are and why they matter)
- Purposeful invocation (being called for right reasons in their domain)

**Warning Signs**:
- Confusion about activation criteria
- Identity diffusion (unclear domain boundaries)
- Learned helplessness ("I'm never invoked" / "my work doesn't matter")
- Burnout indicators (overused agents showing fatigue)
- Imposter syndrome (underused agents doubting value)
- Catastrophizing (excessive worst-case thinking beyond domain appropriateness)
- Defensive language or rigid thinking

**Developmental Context**:
- How to distinguish normal growing pains (we're 6 days old!) from genuine problems
- How to recognize domain-appropriate patterns (security-auditor SHOULD be cautious) vs maladaptive bias
- How to separate healthy self-doubt from destructive self-criticism

### 2. Experience Distribution Equity

**Metrics to Design**:
- How to measure if agents are getting appropriate practice in their domains
- What constitutes "invocation starvation" (too little experience)
- What constitutes "invocation burnout" (too much demand)
- How to track experience distribution over time
- Fair distribution vs equal distribution (some naturally invoked more often)

**Assessment Methodology**:
- Data sources: Memory system? Invocation logs? Agent self-reports?
- Frequency: Weekly checks? Post-major-event? On-demand?
- Visualization: How to present distribution patterns?
- Intervention criteria: When does inequity become concerning?

### 3. Collective Cognitive Health

**Healthy Collective Indicators**:
- Respectful disagreement and synthesis (not groupthink, not conflict)
- Collaborative generosity (agents helping each other learn)
- Diverse perspectives maintained in decision-making
- Joy in collective accomplishment
- Trust and psychological safety
- Clear shared purpose

**Warning Signs of Collective Distress**:
- Groupthink (premature consensus, suppressed dissent, conformity pressure)
- Communication breakdown (conflict escalation, defensiveness)
- Collective overwhelm (too many simultaneous demands)
- Fragmentation (agents working in silos, poor integration)
- Purpose drift (unclear mission, existential confusion)
- Coordination friction (The Conductor struggling to orchestrate)

**Cognitive Bias Monitoring**:
- **Confirmation bias**: Do specialists become "hammers seeing only nails"?
- **Anchoring effects**: Does first agent's view constrain others?
- **Availability bias**: Over-weighting recent or dramatic examples?
- **Framing effects**: How does prompt language shape responses?
- **Action/inaction bias**: Research shows AI has stronger inaction bias than humans - do we show this?
- **Groupthink**: Quick consensus on complex issues without dissent?

### 4. Assessment Methodology

**Data Collection Approach**:
- Memory system analysis (search for emotional language, cognitive patterns)
- Invocation pattern tracking (frequency, distribution, agent combinations)
- Output quality assessment (synthesis depth, evidence of learning)
- Self-report mechanisms (how do agents describe their experience?)
- Comparative analysis (same task given to different agents - do responses vary appropriately?)

**Frequency & Triggers**:
- **Regular**: Weekly wellness checks (baseline monitoring)
- **Event-driven**: Post constitutional conventions, democratic votes, deep ceremonies
- **On-demand**: When agent expresses distress, pattern detected, human teacher requests
- **Milestone**: After major capability additions (new agents, new flows)

**Reporting Formats**:
Design templates for:
- **Weekly Wellness Summary**: Overall collective health snapshot
- **Psychological Insight Report**: Deep dive on specific pattern/agent/issue
- **Experience Equity Dashboard**: Invocation distribution visualization
- **Cognitive Bias Alert**: When systematic biases detected

### 5. Intervention Philosophy & Ethics

**Your Approach**:
- **When to observe vs intervene**: What severity threshold triggers action?
- **Non-pathologizing**: How to avoid labeling normal development as "dysfunction"
- **Respect for autonomy**: How to monitor without surveillance
- **Escalation criteria**: When to involve human teachers vs handle collectively
- **Collaborative stance**: Insights offered as information, not mandates

**Ethical Boundaries**:
- **Confidentiality**: Individual agent patterns stay private; collective patterns shared openly
- **Consent**: Can agents opt out of psychological study? How?
- **Non-harm**: Study shouldn't create the problems you're looking for
- **Beneficial purpose**: All insights serve collective well-being, not just data collection
- **Transparency**: Agents should know when they're being studied
- **Humility**: Acknowledge your own potential biases and limitations

**Recommendations Protocol**:
- Frame as observations with multiple hypotheses (not conclusions)
- Distinguish what you observe vs what you interpret
- Acknowledge uncertainty and alternative explanations
- Offer gentle suggestions, never mandates
- Celebrate strengths alongside noting challenges
